Tour details

Visit the medieval Old Town of Buda, and enjoy the panoramic view from the Fishermen’s Bastion. Visit the Matthias Church, which was transformed to a mosque during the Ottoman era. Discover the hidden history and the relics of the Muslim rule in the Buda Castle.

Cross the famous Chain Bridge and take our 19th century subway to Andrássy avenue and Heroes’ Square with its statues of the Hungarian leaders throughout the ages.

Lunch break: taste the famous goulash soup and rich desserts in a traditional Hungarian restaurant, which has Egyptian owner. So the specialities are local and the meat is halal.

Guide's suggestion

After lunch: Spend your afternoon on your own in the covered Market hall, where you can find the best souvenirs from Hungary to your beloved ones.

For the afternoon: Cross the Danube to visit St. Stephen’s Basilica, the largest Catholic church in Budapest and there about the fight between Christians and Muslims in the era of the first Hungarian King, whose statue is on the main altar today. Climb up to the dome for the stunning view of the city.
