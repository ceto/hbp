Among friends in Hungary

You might think there are so few Muslims in this beautiful Eastern-European capital, that you can not find a mosque to pray and a restaurant to eat halal meat? You are wrong. You can pray in mosques. You can eat halal food. You can even visit one of our famous baths without seeing what you do not want to see.

And… You can have a Muslim guide! Our professional, experienced and licensed guides are all locals, who can not wait to show you the beauty of Budapest

Don't miss the higlights

With your new friend discover the jewels of our city including two UNESCO World Heritage areas. We have a few recommendations:

  • On the top of Gellért Hill get a panoramic view on the city and take a walk around the Citadel to see the Statue of Liberty
  • In the Buda Castle let us visit the Royal Palace and Matthias Church, which was operating as a mosque during the Ottoman era.
  • Parliament of Hungary, Liberty Square and St. Stephens Basilica
  • Let us hop on the oldest subway of the continent to discover Andrássy Avenue, Heroes’ Square and the City Park. We will not forget to stop at the Opera House

Islamic sights of Budapest

Our guide can give you an insight into the daily life of Hungarian Muslims today. We provide special programs that you can't find in a travel guide.

  • With his/her help you can visit our local masjids included Budapest Mosque, the biggest Islamic Center of Eastern-Europe, the oldest appartment mosque of Budapest and many more
  • Discover the Ottoman Heritage of the Buda Castle: Monument of Abdurrahman Pasha and many more…
  • Visit the freshly renovated Tomb of Gül Baba and some of the Turkish Baths that are still operating in the city
